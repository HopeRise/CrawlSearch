In chemistry, it applies to molecules made of five monomers. In biochemistry, it applies to macromolecules, particularly pentameric proteins, made of five protein sub-units. In microbiology, a pentamer is one of the proteins that compose the polyhedral protein shell that encloses the bacterial micro-compartments known as carboxysomes. In immunology, an MHC pentamer is a reagent used to detect antigen-specific CD8+ T cells.
